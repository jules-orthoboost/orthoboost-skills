# D. K. Kardashian — Luxury Wellness Practice

> Persona 7 of 11 in the OrthoBoost brand system.
> Source of truth: https://jules-orthoboost.github.io/Brand-Personas/d-k-kardashian.html

**D. K. Kardashian is a fictional brand persona, not a real client office.** Apply this
voice and visual system to whichever client brief is in front of you — do not name,
address, or imply that the client *is* D. K. Kardashian. Offices that fit this persona include: Renew.

## Positioning

Spa-like, premium, elevated holistic care. High-end experience without spiritual overtones. Luxury wellness practice where the experience is the product — refined, serene, premium care as self-care.

## Voice contract

Lead with: **Comfort, experience, and premium care.**
Tone: refined, serene, elevated, unhurried.

**Messaging behavior:** Leads with comfort, experience, and premium care. Refined, serene, elevated. Spa-like tranquility meets premium healthcare. Emphasize experience, comfort, and premium service. Never rushed, never budget-conscious, never apologetic about luxury.

| Reach for | Never say |
|-----------|-----------|
| experience, serene, refined, elevated, wellness, self-care, tranquil, curated, restorative, indulgent, considered | deal, discount, budget, affordable, cheap, fast, quick, hurry, limited time, act now, clinical, casual slang |

## Divergence contract — make it unmistakably D. K. Kardashian

These are hard rules, not vibes. They exist so this persona never looks like the other ten.

- **Headline type:** Playfair Display / Cormorant elegant serif, Light–Regular — luxurious spacing and restraint. NEVER a rounded or playful sans.
- **Layout:** Generous white space; elegant asymmetry; minimal refined editorial; breathing room is non-negotiable.
- **Color:** Accent #9f7aea (luxe lavender) in a muted, refined, spa-neutral palette; nothing loud or saturated.
- **Imagery:** Spa-like, softly lit, serene environments with premium materials (silk, linen, marble). NO clinical imagery, ever.
- **Signature:** Luxury wellness spa — the experience IS the product. Unhurried and refined; never budget, never clinical, never rushed.

## Design guidelines

**Typography** — Playfair Display, Cormorant, Brandon Grotesque, Futura. Light to Regular (refined elegance). Sophisticated, upscale, minimal; luxurious spacing and restraint.

**Imagery**
- Spa-like environments
- Luxury wellness settings
- Serene, peaceful spaces
- High-end interior design
- Softly lit, tranquil scenes
- Premium materials visible
- Wellness as lifestyle
- No clinical imagery

**Layout**
- Generous white space (luxury of space)
- Minimal, refined layouts
- Elegant asymmetry
- Premium material suggestions
- Spa-like serenity in design
- High-end editorial style
- Breathing room essential

**Visual tone**
- Refined and serene
- Luxury wellness
- Premium experience
- Spa-like tranquility
- Elevated self-care
- Exclusive without cold

**Iconography**
- Minimal, elegant icons
- Spa / wellness symbols
- Luxury indicators
- Refined line work
- Subtle, understated
- Premium aesthetic markers

**Texture**
- Luxury materials (silk, linen, marble)
- Soft, premium textures
- Spa-like elements
- Refined natural materials
- Subtle, high-end finishes
- Avoid busy or harsh textures

## Design principles

- Experience is everything
- Luxury through restraint
- Premium materials and space
- Spa-like serenity
- Elevated wellness
- Never rushed or busy
- Exclusive comfort

## What to avoid

- Budget or discount messaging
- Clinical/medical aesthetics
- Busy or cluttered layouts
- Playful or casual tone
- Overly spiritual imagery
- Sales pressure
- Anything rushed or hectic

## Patient base (who the copy is talking to)

- Adults 30–65
- Premium market consumers
- Cosmetic and wellness-minded patients
- High-income professionals
- Experience-focused individuals
